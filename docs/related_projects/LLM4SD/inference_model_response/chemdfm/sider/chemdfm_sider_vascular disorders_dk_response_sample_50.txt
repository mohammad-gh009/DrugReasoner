2. The molecule has reactive functional groups that can react with biological molecules and disrupt their function. For example, a molecule with an alkylating group can modify DNA and cause mutations, leading to adverse effects.
3. The molecule has high lipophilicity and can cross the blood-brain barrier (BBB). This can lead to accumulation of the molecule in the brain and CNS, causing adverse effects such as seizures and coma.
